AUTOMATED EMERGENCY RESPONSE SYSTEM VIA SATELLITE

Abstract:

Road accidents and emergencies in remote or low-connectivity areas often face delayed responses as traditional systems relying on cellular networks fail in such regions, leaving victims vulnerable. This system leverages satellite communication, providing seamless, automated emergency alerts and real-time GPS tracking to ensure rapid notification to responders and emergency contacts even in areas without cellular coverage. Specifically, it addresses the critical need for dependable communication during accidents, medical emergencies, and disasters, enhancing safety and reducing response times, particularly in underserved and high-risk locations. It automatically detects severe impacts like airbag deployment or allows manual emergency reporting via dashboard controls. Moreover, using map APIs or government data, it identifies the nearest responders and notifies them through multi-channel alerts, including SMS and voice messages. Finally, the data collected is analyzed using data analytics to identify high-accident regions and share this information with respective departments (like NHAI).
